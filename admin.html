How This Dashboard Works
GPS Tracking
Each shuttle has a MapsIndoors GPS tracker that broadcasts its location every few seconds. The "GPS" tag on each shuttle means we're receiving a live signal. If a shuttle shows no GPS, the hardware may be off or out of range.
Driver App
Drivers use a mobile app (/driver) that shows their route and stops. When a driver has the app open, you'll see a "DRV" tag on their shuttle. The app reports the driver's current stop, lap count, and route type (day/night) every 30 seconds.
Automated Event Logging
The main tracker automatically detects when a shuttle arrives at, departs from, or skips a stop based on GPS proximity. These events feed into the stats, performance tables, and lap tracking below. No manual input needed.
Day vs Night Route
At 7 PM CT the route automatically switches from Day (11 stops) to Night (13 stops), adding Hotel Vegas and Shangri-La. The route chip in the top bar shows which mode is currently active.
